The word "chrome" itself is synonymous with brilliant reflectivity. ## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 7.19 g/cm³ — dense and heavy
- **Reactivity:** Extremely resistant to tarnishing thanks to a thin protective oxide layer
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** High melting point of 1,907°C
- **Appearance:** Silvery with a distinctive bluish tinge; takes the highest mirror polish of any metal
- **Other:** Named from the Greek word "chroma" meaning color, because its compounds come in many vivid colors (green, orange, yellow, red)

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Chromium is named after the Greek word for "color" because its compounds make some of the most vivid pigments — emeralds are green because of chromium, and rubies are red because of it too!
2. That shiny mirror finish on car bumpers, bathroom faucets, and motorcycle parts? That's a thin coating of chromium — just 0.001 inches thick but almost impossible to scratch.
3. Chromium is one of the hardest pure metals — a 6.5 on the Mohs hardness scale, harder than steel and almost as hard as a gemstone.
