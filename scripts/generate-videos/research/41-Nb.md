## Why This Concept
Niobium is one of the few metals that can be anodized into a complete rainbow of vivid colors without any dye or paint — just by varying the voltage applied. The color comes from thin-film interference in the oxide layer, the same physics that creates rainbow soap bubbles. Watching a dull grey metal transform into vivid purple, blue, or green in real time is genuinely magical.

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 8.57 g/cm3
- **Reactivity:** Very corrosion-resistant; does not react with air at room temperature
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at 2477C, boils at 4744C — extremely high
- **Appearance:** Lustrous grey metal that can be anodized into any color of the rainbow
- **Other:** A superconductor at low temperatures; used in MRI machines and particle accelerators

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Niobium can turn any color of the rainbow — purple, blue, green, gold — just by running electricity through it, with no paint or dye needed
2. The Large Hadron Collider at CERN uses niobium superconducting magnets to smash atoms together at nearly the speed of light
3. Niobium is named after Niobe, a figure from Greek mythology who was the daughter of Tantalus (tantalum is element 73) — they are literally parent and child on the periodic table
