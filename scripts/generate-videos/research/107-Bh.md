## Why This Concept
Bohrium's story is primarily about the GSI laboratory in Darmstadt, Germany, which discovered elements 107-112. There is nothing physically observable about bohrium — it exists only as a fleeting detection event in a particle physics experiment. The element's properties are almost entirely theoretical.

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Half-life:** Most stable confirmed isotope (270Bh) lasts about 2.4 minutes. An unconfirmed isotope (278Bh) may last about 11.5 minutes.
- **How it was made:** First synthesized in 1981 at GSI Darmstadt, Germany, by Peter Armbruster and Gottfried Munzenberg. They bombarded bismuth-209 with chromium-54 ions. Earlier Soviet claims from 1976 were not confirmed.
- **Predicted properties:** Expected to behave like rhenium (group 7). Very little experimental chemistry has been done.
- **Other:** Named after Niels Bohr, one of the founders of quantum mechanics and atomic theory. GSI Darmstadt discovered six elements (107-112) — the most prolific element-discovery lab since the Berkeley era.

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Bohrium is named after Niels Bohr, the physicist who figured out how electrons orbit the nucleus in specific energy levels — basically the person who explained why atoms look the way they do.
2. It was discovered at a lab in Germany called GSI that went on an incredible element-discovering streak, finding six new elements (107 through 112) between 1981 and 1996.
3. Only a handful of bohrium atoms have ever existed in human history, and each one vanished within minutes. You have never been in the same room as a bohrium atom — almost certainly no one ever will be.
