## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up of a graphite crucible filled with molten silver heated to over 960 degrees Celsius — the liquid metal glows with an intense, warm orange-white radiance. The molten silver is poured in a smooth, heavy stream into a mold, flowing like liquid mercury but glowing with fierce heat. The stream catches the light with impossibly perfect reflections — a river of liquid mirror. As it fills the mold and begins to cool, the surface transitions from glowing orange to warm gold, then to the iconic brilliant white-silver with the highest reflectivity of any element. The cooling surface develops a flawless mirror finish that reflects the world around it with crystal clarity. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Silver has the highest electrical and thermal conductivity and the highest reflectivity of any element. Seeing it transform from a glowing orange-white molten liquid into its iconic mirror-bright solid form showcases both its high melting point and its legendary luster. The pour creates dynamic motion, and the mirror finish is immediately recognizable.

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 10.49 g/cm3 — noticeably heavy in the hand
- **Reactivity:** Tarnishes in sulfur-containing air to form black silver sulfide; noble to most acids
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at 961.8C, boils at 2162C
- **Appearance:** Brilliant white metallic luster — highest reflectivity of all elements (97%)
- **Other:** Best electrical and thermal conductor of all elements; used in mirrors, electronics, and medicine

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Silver is the best conductor of electricity and heat of ALL elements — even better than copper or gold
2. Silver reflects 97% of visible light, more than any other element — that is why the best mirrors are coated in silver
3. The chemical symbol "Ag" comes from the Latin word "argentum" — Argentina the country was named after silver because Spanish explorers thought it was full of the metal
