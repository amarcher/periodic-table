## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up of small glass dishes containing europium oxide powder in a dark room. A UV lamp switches on, and the powder instantly erupts with an intense, brilliant red phosphorescence — a deep, saturated crimson glow that illuminates everything nearby. Next to it, a europium-doped strontium aluminate sample blazes with electric blue-green light. The UV lamp switches off, but the europium compounds keep glowing — the red fades slowly while the blue-green persists with an ethereal, ghostly luminescence that refuses to die. The brightest, longest-lasting glow-in-the-dark material known to science. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Europium is THE phosphor element — it produces the most brilliant red phosphorescence of any element and is responsible for the red in TV screens, fluorescent lights, and LED displays. Its ability to glow under UV light and continue glowing afterward (phosphorescence vs fluorescence) is visually stunning and immediately captivating for kids. The vivid red and blue colors against a dark background are extremely photogenic.

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 5.24 g/cm3 — the least dense lanthanide
- **Reactivity:** Most reactive rare earth; oxidizes rapidly in air, reacts with water like calcium
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at 822C, boils at 1529C
- **Appearance:** Soft silvery metal; compounds glow vivid red (Eu3+) or blue (Eu2+) under UV light
- **Other:** Europium is used in anti-counterfeiting features in euro banknotes — it glows under UV blacklight

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Europium makes the red color in old TV screens and modern LED displays — without it, screens could not show true red
2. Euro banknotes contain tiny amounts of europium that glow red under UV light to prove they are real
3. Europium-doped powder is the brightest, longest-lasting glow-in-the-dark material ever discovered — it can glow for hours after being charged with light
