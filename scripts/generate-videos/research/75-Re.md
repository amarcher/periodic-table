## Why This Concept
Rhenium's key properties — high melting point (3,180°C, second only to tungsten), extreme density, and corrosion resistance — are impressive on paper but do not produce unique visual effects that would differentiate it from other refractory metals in an 8-second video. It does not glow distinctively, react dramatically, or have unusual physical states.

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 21.02 g/cm3 — fourth densest element, exceeded only by osmium, iridium, and platinum
- **Reactivity:** Resists corrosion well, slowly tarnishes in moist air
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Second highest melting point of any element at 3,180°C, after tungsten; boiling point 5,596°C (highest of any element!)
- **Appearance:** Silvery-white, lustrous, very hard metal
- **Other:** One of the rarest elements in Earth's crust — last stable element to be discovered (1925)

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Rhenium was the last stable, naturally occurring element to be discovered — scientists did not find it until 1925
2. It has the highest boiling point of any element — you would need to heat it to 5,596°C to turn it into a gas
3. Rhenium is so rare that the entire world only produces about 50 tons per year — you could fit a year's supply in a couple of dump trucks
