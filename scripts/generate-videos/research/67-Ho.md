## Why This Concept
Holmium has the highest magnetic moment of any naturally occurring element (10.6 Bohr magnetons), but this magnetic property is invisible at room temperature since holmium only becomes ferromagnetic below -254C. Its compounds have distinctive brownish-yellow and pink colors, but these are subtle rather than dramatic. The holmium laser (2.1 micron, infrared) is invisible to the human eye. Overall, holmium lacks a single visually explosive demonstration for an 8-second video.

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 8.80 g/cm3
- **Reactivity:** Slowly tarnishes in moist air; stable in dry air; reacts with water
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at 1474C, boils at 2700C
- **Appearance:** Silvery-white metal; compounds are distinctively brownish-yellow to pink
- **Other:** Highest magnetic moment of any naturally occurring element (10.6 Bohr magnetons)

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Holmium has the strongest magnetic field of any single atom in nature — it is the ultimate atomic magnet
2. Holmium lasers are used by doctors to blast apart kidney stones inside patients' bodies without any surgery
3. Holmium was named after Stockholm, Sweden (Holmia in Latin) — the city where it was discovered in 1878
