# Cerium (Ce) — Element 58

**Confidence:** High
**Video concept:** A ferrocerium rod being struck, unleashing a spectacular shower of white-hot sparks reaching 3000C

## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up slow-motion of a steel striker scraping along a ferrocerium rod in a dark room. An explosive shower of brilliant white-hot sparks erupts from the contact point, each spark reaching temperatures of 3000 degrees Celsius, trailing bright orange-white streaks through the darkness. The sparks fan outward in a dramatic arc, some bouncing and splitting into smaller fragments on impact. The cerium-rich alloy sheds incandescent particles that glow intensely as they oxidize mid-flight. A cascade of thousands of tiny burning metal fragments lighting up the darkness. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Cerium has an exceptionally low ignition temperature (150-180C) which is why it is the key ingredient in ferrocerium "flint" strikers. Every lighter, fire starter, and survival tool uses cerium's pyrophoric nature. The shower of sparks is one of the most universally recognized chemical phenomena — every kid has seen a lighter spark. Showing it in slow-motion extreme close-up reveals the beautiful physics of thousands of tiny metal particles burning at 3000C+.

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 6.77 g/cm3
- **Reactivity:** Ignites at just 150-180C — the lowest ignition temperature of any metallic element; burns brilliantly white
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at 798C, boils at 3443C
- **Appearance:** Silvery-white metal with a slight yellowish tinge; tarnishes rapidly in air
- **Other:** Most abundant of all rare earth elements — more common in Earth's crust than copper or lead

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Every time someone flicks a lighter, cerium makes the spark — it ignites at just 150C, lower than any other metal
2. Cerium is the most abundant rare earth element and is actually more common than copper in Earth's crust
3. Cerium sparks burn at over 3000C (5400F) — that is hotter than the surface of many stars
