## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up of a stream of pale, translucent blue liquid oxygen being slowly poured from a small Dewar flask between the poles of a large horseshoe magnet. As the liquid passes between the magnet poles, it defies gravity — caught and suspended in mid-air by the magnetic field, forming a shimmering blue bridge of liquid hanging impossibly between the silvery metal poles. The trapped liquid oxygen glows with a delicate sky-blue color, wobbling and rippling slightly. Wisps of cold condensation fog curl around the suspended liquid. More liquid is added and the blue pool grows, held aloft by invisible magnetic force. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Liquid oxygen being suspended by a magnet is one of the most surprising and visually stunning demonstrations in chemistry. Most people do not know oxygen is magnetic, and the sight of a blue liquid hovering in mid-air between magnet poles is genuinely jaw-dropping. The beautiful pale blue color of liquid oxygen adds visual richness. It demonstrates an invisible property (paramagnetism) in a visible, dramatic way.

## Alternative Video Concepts Considered
- **Steel wool burning in pure oxygen**: Dramatic but focuses on the iron, not oxygen itself
- **Liquid oxygen pouring (no magnet)**: Shows the blue color but lacks the dramatic "wow" moment
- **Oxygen supporting combustion**: A classic demo but the oxygen itself is invisible — the visual is about whatever is burning

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** Liquid oxygen is 1.141 g/cm3 — denser than water
- **Reactivity:** Highly reactive oxidizer; supports combustion of almost all materials
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Boils at -183C; freezes at -218C
- **Appearance:** Colorless and invisible as a gas; liquid oxygen is a striking pale blue color
- **Other:** Paramagnetic — attracted to magnets due to two unpaired electrons; the only common liquid that is magnetic

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Liquid oxygen is blue — not the faint blue of the sky, but a real, visible pale blue liquid you can pour
2. Oxygen is magnetic — if you pour liquid oxygen between the poles of a strong magnet, it will stick there and hang in mid-air
3. You breathe in about 550 liters of pure oxygen every single day — without it, your brain would shut down in just 4 minutes
