## Why This Concept
Darmstadtium does not have a particularly distinctive story compared to its neighbors. It was discovered at GSI Darmstadt (along with five other elements), it exists for seconds, and its chemistry is entirely theoretical. There is nothing unique enough to warrant a video.

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Half-life:** Most stable isotope (281Ds) lasts about 14 seconds. It decays by spontaneous fission 85% of the time and alpha decay 15% of the time.
- **How it was made:** First synthesized in November 1994 at GSI Darmstadt, Germany, by Peter Armbruster and Gottfried Munzenberg. They bombarded lead-208 with nickel-62 ions.
- **Predicted properties:** Expected to behave like platinum (group 10). No chemical experiments have been performed. Predicted to be a very dense solid metal.
- **Other:** Named after Darmstadt, Germany, where GSI is located. GSI also has a convention center called "Darmstadtium" — possibly the only building named after a chemical element that was discovered in the same city.

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Darmstadtium is named after Darmstadt, a city in Germany where scientists discovered SIX new elements between 1981 and 1996 — making it arguably the element capital of the world.
2. There is a conference center in Darmstadt, Germany, that is actually called "Darmstadtium" — it might be the only building in the world named after a chemical element discovered in the same city.
3. Darmstadtium should behave like platinum if we could ever make enough to test, but since the longest any atom has lasted is about 14 seconds, we may never know for sure.
