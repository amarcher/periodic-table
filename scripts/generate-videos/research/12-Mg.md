# Magnesium (Mg) — Element 12

## Video Recommendation
**Should generate video:** Yes
**Confidence:** High
**Video concept:** A strip of magnesium ribbon igniting and burning with a blindingly brilliant white flame, showering sparks

## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up slow-motion of a strip of silvery magnesium ribbon being ignited from one end. The flame starts as a flickering red-orange, then erupts into an intensely brilliant white-hot blaze that consumes the ribbon, radiating dazzling white light in all directions. Tiny white-hot sparks and glowing fragments of magnesium oxide drift away from the burning tip. The ribbon curls and crumbles into delicate white ash as the blazing white flame creeps steadily along its length, illuminating swirling wisps of white smoke. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Magnesium burning is one of the most iconic chemistry demonstrations ever — the flame reaches over 3,100 degrees Celsius and emits light so intense it can temporarily blind you. The brilliant white flame is visually unmatched among common metal reactions, and the slow consumption of the ribbon creates a natural dramatic progression perfect for an 8-second clip.

## Alternative Video Concepts Considered
- **Magnesium in dry ice (CO2) reaction**: Spectacular but requires a complex setup that would be hard to convey visually in 8 seconds
- **Magnesium dissolving in acid with hydrogen bubbles**: Less visually dramatic than the burning — just fizzing rather than blinding white light
- **Magnesium flash powder ignition**: Too fast (milliseconds) to fill 8 seconds meaningfully

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** Very lightweight metal — only 1.74 g/cm3, about 2/3 the density of aluminum
- **Reactivity:** Burns in air with a brilliant white flame at over 3,100 C; can even burn in carbon dioxide and nitrogen
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at 650 C, boils at 1,091 C
- **Appearance:** Shiny silvery-white metal that tarnishes slightly in air to a dull gray
- **Other:** Approximately 10% of the combustion energy is released as visible light — the highest efficiency of any known chemical light source

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Magnesium burns so bright that it was used as the first camera flash — early photographers literally set magnesium powder on fire to take pictures
2. Your body contains about 25 grams of magnesium right now, and you need it for over 300 different chemical reactions in your cells
3. Magnesium is the eighth most abundant element in Earth's crust and the third most abundant element dissolved in seawater
