# Carbon (C) — Element 6

## Video Recommendation
**Should generate video:** Yes
**Confidence:** High
**Video concept:** A diamond heated red-hot and dropped into liquid oxygen, burning with brilliant incandescent white light

## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up of a small rough diamond held in metal tongs, glowing bright cherry-red after being heated by a torch. The incandescent diamond is lowered into a glass flask of pure liquid oxygen. On contact, the diamond erupts into a dazzling, blindingly bright white-hot combustion, burning with fierce intensity like a tiny star. The diamond shrinks visibly as pure carbon combines with oxygen, releasing brilliant white sparks and an intense glow that illuminates the entire flask. The liquid oxygen bubbles and churns around the burning gem. The diamond slowly vanishes, consumed entirely by the reaction. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Burning a diamond is one of the most dramatic demonstrations in chemistry — proving that the world's most prized gemstone is just carbon. The visual of an indestructible-seeming diamond being consumed by brilliant white fire is shocking and unforgettable. It combines the "wow" factor of destroying something precious with genuinely spectacular combustion visuals.

## Alternative Video Concepts Considered
- **Graphite in liquid oxygen**: Also burns dramatically but lacks the narrative punch of a diamond
- **Carbon arc lamp**: Extremely bright but more industrial than scientific
- **Diamond vs graphite side-by-side**: Interesting conceptually but not enough visual action for 8 seconds
- **Charcoal burning**: Too ordinary and mundane

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** Varies wildly by form: diamond (3.51 g/cm3) vs graphite (2.27 g/cm3) — same element, different density
- **Reactivity:** Burns in oxygen to form CO2; diamond requires high temperatures but then burns vigorously
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Highest melting point of any element (3550C for diamond at high pressure); sublimes at 3642C
- **Appearance:** Exists in radically different forms: transparent diamond, black graphite, hollow fullerenes, cylindrical nanotubes
- **Other:** The basis of all known life; forms more compounds than any other element except hydrogen

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Diamonds can burn — if heated hot enough in pure oxygen, a diamond will catch fire and vanish completely, turning into carbon dioxide gas
2. The graphite in your pencil and the diamond in a ring are made of the exact same element — just arranged differently
3. Carbon is the backbone of every living thing on Earth — your body is about 18% carbon by weight
