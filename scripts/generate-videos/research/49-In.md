# Indium (In) — Element 49

## Video Recommendation
**Should generate video:** Yes
**Confidence:** Medium
**Video concept:** An indium metal bar being slowly bent, producing its eerie crackling "cry" while the ultra-soft metal deforms like putty

## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up of a silvery-white bar of indium metal being slowly bent by gloved hands. The incredibly soft metal yields and deforms like metallic putty, bending far more easily than any typical metal. As it bends, the surface crinkles and wrinkles, and you can almost hear the eerie high-pitched crackling "cry" — caused by crystal planes snapping and reorganizing inside the metal. The bar is then pressed against a glass surface, leaving a visible metallic streak like a silver pencil mark. Finally, the indium is placed on a warm hotplate at just 157 degrees Celsius, where it quickly melts into a shimmering pool of liquid metal that clings to and wets the glass surface beneath it. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Indium's extreme softness (Mohs 1.2), its haunting "cry" when bent, and its remarkably low melting point combine to create a multi-sensory experience. The visual of a metal bending like putty, marking glass like a pencil, and then melting at modest temperatures is surprising and counterintuitive — metal is not supposed to behave this way. Kids will find this "weird metal" fascinating.

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 7.31 g/cm3
- **Reactivity:** Stable in air; reacts slowly with oxygen to form a thin oxide film
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at 156.6C — very low; boils at 2072C
- **Appearance:** Silvery-white, extremely soft metal (Mohs 1.2) that can be cut with a fingernail
- **Other:** Makes an eerie crackling "cry" when bent; wets glass when molten; stays soft even at cryogenic temperatures

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Indium is so soft you can scratch it with your fingernail and draw with it on paper like a silver crayon
2. When you bend an indium bar, it makes a spooky high-pitched crackling sound called an "indium cry" as crystals snap inside
3. Every touchscreen on every phone and tablet uses a thin invisible coating of indium tin oxide — without indium, your phone screen would not respond to your finger
