# Tellurium (Te) — Element 52

## Video Recommendation
**Should generate video:** Yes
**Confidence:** Medium
**Video concept:** Crystalline tellurium being heated and burning with a vivid blue-green flame, producing white tellurium dioxide smoke

## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up of a chunk of crystalline tellurium with a striking silvery-white metallic luster, sitting on a ceramic plate. A flame is applied and the brittle metalloid begins to burn with a distinctive, vivid blue-green flame that flickers and dances. White smoke of tellurium dioxide billows upward from the burning surface in thick, swirling plumes. The blue-green fire reflects off the crystalline facets of the remaining tellurium, creating an otherworldly glow. The brittle crystal structure causes small pieces to crack and pop off as the heat penetrates, each fragment igniting with its own tiny blue-green flame. The contrast between the metallic silver crystal and the alien blue-green fire is mesmerizing. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Tellurium burns with a distinctive blue-green flame that is visually unique and alien-looking. Combined with the white smoke and the metallic crystalline appearance of the starting material, this creates a visually compelling sequence. The blue-green flame color is rare and unexpected, immediately distinguishing this from other burning-metal videos.

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 6.24 g/cm3
- **Reactivity:** Burns in air with a blue-green flame; brittle and easily pulverized
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at 449.5C, boils at 988C — highest of the chalcogens
- **Appearance:** Silvery-white crystalline metalloid with a metallic luster; brittle
- **Other:** Touching even a tiny amount makes your breath smell like garlic for weeks

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. If even a microscopic amount of tellurium gets on your skin, your breath will smell like garlic for weeks — and there is nothing you can do about it
2. Tellurium burns with a spooky blue-green flame that looks like alien fire
3. Tellurium is a semiconductor that becomes a better conductor of electricity when you shine light on it — it can "see" light
