# Nickel (Ni) — Element 28

## Video Recommendation
**Should generate video:** Yes
**Confidence:** Medium
**Video concept:** A polished nickel sphere being powerfully attracted to a strong magnet, demonstrating its ferromagnetic properties

## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up of a highly polished nickel metal sphere with a warm silvery-gold luster resting on a dark glass surface. A powerful neodymium magnet slowly approaches from above. Suddenly the nickel sphere snaps upward and locks onto the magnet with a satisfying click, defying gravity. The camera captures the sphere hanging suspended from the magnet, rotating slowly to reveal its beautiful warm-toned metallic surface. More polished nickel spheres are brought near and they leap from the surface one by one, chaining together beneath the magnet in a cascading magnetic attraction. The warm golden-silver sheen of the nickel catches the light as the spheres swing gently. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Nickel is one of only four elements that are ferromagnetic at room temperature, which makes for a visually dramatic demonstration when combined with a strong magnet. Its distinctive warm silvery color with a slight golden tinge sets it apart from iron and cobalt visually. The snap of magnetic attraction is instantly engaging and the suspended, gravity-defying spheres create a sense of wonder.

## Alternative Video Concepts Considered
- **Nickel plating process:** Interesting but industrial and complex, hard to make visually dramatic
- **Polished nickel reflecting light:** Beautiful but too static for an 8-second video
- **Nickel dissolving in acid (green solution):** Slow and the green color isn't unique enough

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 8.91 g/cm³ — dense and heavy
- **Reactivity:** Resistant to corrosion; forms a thin protective oxide layer
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at 1,455°C
- **Appearance:** Silvery-white with a distinctive slight golden tinge; takes a high polish
- **Other:** One of only four ferromagnetic elements at room temperature; loses magnetism above 355°C (its Curie temperature)

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Nickel is one of only four elements that a magnet will stick to at room temperature — iron, cobalt, nickel, and gadolinium are the only ones in the whole periodic table!
2. The U.S. "nickel" coin actually has more copper than nickel — it's 75% copper and only 25% nickel, but it gets its name from the nickel that gives it a silvery color.
3. Nickel is believed to have come from outer space — some of the nickel on Earth arrived in meteorites, and the Earth's core is made of iron and nickel swirling around together.
