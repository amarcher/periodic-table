# Lithium (Li) — Element 3

## Video Recommendation
**Should generate video:** Yes
**Confidence:** High
**Video concept:** Lithium metal dropped into water producing a vivid crimson-red flame on the surface

## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up slow-motion of a small silvery chunk of lithium metal being dropped into a glass dish of water. On contact, the metal hisses and fizzes, releasing streams of tiny hydrogen bubbles. The lithium piece dances across the water surface, gradually shrinking as it reacts. The escaping hydrogen ignites with a stunning deep crimson-red flame that flickers and pulses above the water, casting rich red reflections across the rippling surface. Wisps of white vapor rise from the hot reaction zone as the metal slowly dissolves. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Lithium's reaction with water is gentler than sodium's but produces the most distinctive and beautiful flame color of any alkali metal — a deep, saturated crimson red that is immediately striking. The combination of the fizzing water reaction with the gorgeous red flame makes for a uniquely colored and dramatic video that stands apart from the sodium video (which features yellow-orange flames).

## Alternative Video Concepts Considered
- **Lithium flame test (wire loop)**: Beautiful crimson color but too small and brief — less dramatic than the water reaction
- **Lithium battery demonstration**: Not about the pure element; too industrial and not visually exciting
- **Cutting lithium metal**: Shows the shiny interior tarnishing, but not dramatic enough for 8 seconds

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** The lightest metal — so light it floats on water (density 0.534 g/cm3, about half the density of water)
- **Reactivity:** Reacts steadily with water; less explosive than sodium or potassium but produces a unique crimson flame
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Lowest melting point of any metal that is solid at room temperature (180C)
- **Appearance:** Soft, silvery-white metal that tarnishes to dark gray within seconds in air
- **Other:** Used in rechargeable batteries that power nearly every smartphone, laptop, and electric car

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Lithium is so light it can float on water — it is the lightest metal in the entire periodic table
2. You can cut lithium with a butter knife because it is softer than most metals
3. The battery in your phone or tablet almost certainly contains lithium — it powers almost all modern rechargeable devices
