Cadmium vapor is a distinctive deep yellow — one of the few metals that produces a vividly colored gas. This is visually unique and immediately eye-catching. Combined with cadmium's bluish-tinged appearance (unusual among metals) and its relatively low boiling point, the metal-to-colored-vapor transformation makes for a compelling visual sequence.

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 8.65 g/cm3
- **Reactivity:** Relatively resistant to corrosion; tarnishes in moist air
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at 321C, boils at 767C — relatively low for a metal
- **Appearance:** Silvery-white with a distinctive bluish tint; soft enough to cut with a knife
- **Other:** Its vapor is deep yellow and monatomic; cadmium compounds produce brilliant yellow, orange, and red pigments

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Cadmium produces a deep yellow vapor when heated — one of the only metals whose gas you can actually see with a vivid color
2. The brilliant yellows, oranges, and reds in many famous paintings (including works by Monet and Van Gogh) come from cadmium pigments
3. Cadmium is extremely toxic — your body cannot get rid of it, so even tiny amounts accumulate over a lifetime with a half-life of 16-30 years
