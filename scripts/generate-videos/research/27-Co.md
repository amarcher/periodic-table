## Why This Concept
Cobalt's most iconic visual property is the extraordinary deep blue color it imparts to glass and ceramics — "cobalt blue" has been a prized pigment for over 4,000 years. While the pure metal itself is an unremarkable silvery-gray, showing cobalt blue glass being worked captures the element's most famous and visually stunning characteristic. The depth and saturation of cobalt blue is unmistakable and captivating.

- **Cobalt compounds in different colors:** Less iconic than the signature cobalt blue

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 8.90 g/cm³ — quite dense and heavy
- **Reactivity:** Relatively stable in air; slowly oxidizes
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** High melting point of 1,495°C
- **Appearance:** Hard, lustrous, silvery-gray metal with a faint bluish tinge
- **Other:** One of only four elements that are ferromagnetic at room temperature (along with iron, nickel, and gadolinium)

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. "Cobalt blue" is one of the most famous colors in art — ancient Egyptians and Chinese potters used cobalt to make stunning blue glass and ceramics over 4,000 years ago!
2. Cobalt is one of only four elements that are naturally magnetic — you can pick it up with a magnet, just like iron.
3. The element gets its name from "kobold," the German word for a mischievous goblin — miners who found cobalt ore thought goblins had cursed their silver because cobalt ore looked like silver but wasn't!
