# Calcium (Ca) — Element 20

## Video Recommendation
**Should generate video:** Yes
**Confidence:** Medium
**Video concept:** Calcium metal reacting with water, producing streams of hydrogen bubbles while the water turns milky white

## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up of a shiny silvery piece of calcium metal placed into a clear glass beaker of water. Tiny bubbles of hydrogen gas immediately begin forming on the metal's surface, growing into a steady stream of rising bubbles. The crystal-clear water gradually turns cloudy and milky white as calcium hydroxide forms, creating swirling white clouds that billow outward from the reacting metal. The bubbling intensifies, with streams of gas percolating upward through the increasingly opaque white liquid. Wisps of white precipitate drift and curl through the water like underwater smoke. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Calcium's water reaction is gentler than potassium or sodium but creates a uniquely beautiful visual effect — the dramatic transformation of clear water into opaque milky-white liquid as insoluble calcium hydroxide forms. The combination of rising hydrogen bubbles and swirling white clouds creates an ethereal, almost magical visual that is both scientifically educational and visually striking.

## Alternative Video Concepts Considered
- **Calcium burning with orange-red flame:** Possible but requires higher temperatures and is less distinctive than the water reaction's milky transformation
- **Calcium metal being cut (showing silvery interior):** Too similar to other alkali earth metals and not dramatic enough
- **Calcium reacting with acid:** More vigorous but less visually unique than the milky-water effect

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 1.55 g/cm³ — relatively light for a metal
- **Reactivity:** Reacts with water slowly but steadily, less violently than alkali metals
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at 842°C — reasonably high melting point
- **Appearance:** Silvery-white metal that quickly develops a dull gray oxide coating in air
- **Other:** Fifth most abundant element in Earth's crust; essential for bones and teeth

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Your bones and teeth are made with calcium — there's about 1 kilogram (2.2 pounds) of calcium in your body right now!
2. When you drop calcium metal in water, the water turns white and milky like magic — that's a new chemical being made right before your eyes.
3. Calcium is what makes limestone, chalk, marble, and seashells — all of those are built from calcium compounds.
