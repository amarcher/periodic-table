# Promethium (Pm) — Element 61

## Video Recommendation
**Should generate video:** Yes
**Confidence:** Medium
**Video concept:** Promethium salt glowing with an eerie pale blue-green light in total darkness from its own radioactivity

## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up of a small sealed glass vial containing promethium salt crystals sitting on a dark surface. The room is pitch black, but the crystals emit an eerie, ghostly pale blue-green glow entirely from their own radioactive decay — no external light source needed. The soft luminescence pulses gently, casting faint blue-green light onto the glass walls of the vial. The glow is ethereal and otherworldly, like a tiny captured piece of radioactive starlight. A self-luminous element that has been glowing since the moment it was created, powered by atomic decay. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Promethium is the only radioactive lanthanide with no stable isotopes, and its salts glow in the dark with an eerie blue-green phosphorescence powered entirely by their own radioactive decay. This self-luminous property is hauntingly beautiful and deeply memorable. The concept of a substance that glows forever without any external energy source captures kids' imaginations and introduces radioactivity in a visual, non-threatening way.

## Alternative Video Concepts Considered
- **Promethium in luminous paint on instrument dials:** Historical but the focus should be on the pure element
- **Promethium nuclear battery:** Too abstract and not visually dramatic
- **Showing radioactive decay tracks:** Requires specialized equipment, less visually appealing

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 7.26 g/cm3 (estimated)
- **Reactivity:** Radioactive; all isotopes are unstable. Most common isotope Pm-145 has a half-life of 17.7 years
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at 1042C, boils at 3000C (estimated)
- **Appearance:** Metallic, silvery-white; salts glow pale blue-green in the dark
- **Other:** Only about 500-600 grams exist naturally in the entire Earth's crust at any given moment

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Promethium salts glow in the dark all by themselves — no batteries, no charging, just pure radioactive energy lighting them up
2. Only about 500 grams of promethium exist naturally in the entire Earth — it has to be made in nuclear reactors
3. It is named after Prometheus, the Greek titan who stole fire from the gods — fitting for an element that makes its own light
