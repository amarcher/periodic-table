## Why This Concept
Mercury is the only metal that is liquid at room temperature, and its visual properties are extraordinary — a perfectly reflective silver mirror that flows like water but is dense enough to float iron. The sight of a solid metal object floating on a liquid metal is genuinely jaw-dropping and immediately communicates mercury's incredible density. This is one of the most visually mesmerizing elements in the entire periodic table.

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 13.5 g/cm3 — so dense that iron, lead, and even cannon balls float on it
- **Reactivity:** Relatively inert but forms amalgams (alloys) with many metals; extremely toxic as vapor
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at -39°C (liquid at room temperature!); boils at 357°C
- **Appearance:** Brilliant, mirror-like silver liquid — the only metal that is liquid at standard conditions
- **Other:** Symbol Hg comes from Latin "hydrargyrum" meaning "liquid silver"; also called quicksilver

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Mercury is the only metal that is a liquid at room temperature — it flows like water but is shiny like a mirror
2. Mercury is so incredibly dense that if you dropped an iron cannonball into a pool of it, the cannonball would float on top
3. Its nickname "quicksilver" comes from how it moves — tiny droplets race around like they are alive, breaking apart and snapping back together
