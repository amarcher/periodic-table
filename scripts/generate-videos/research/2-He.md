# Helium (He) — Element 2

## Video Recommendation
**Should generate video:** Yes
**Confidence:** Medium
**Video concept:** Superfluid liquid helium creeping up and over the walls of a glass beaker, defying gravity

## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up of a small glass beaker filled with crystal-clear liquid helium cooled below 2 Kelvin. The superfluid begins creeping up the inside walls of the glass as a thin, glistening film, silently climbing over the rim and flowing down the outside of the beaker in defiance of gravity. Droplets form at the bottom of the beaker and drip steadily downward like an impossible inverted fountain. The liquid surface inside is perfectly still — zero viscosity, zero turbulence. Faint condensation fog curls around the ultra-cold glass. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Superfluid helium is one of the most mind-bending phenomena in physics — a liquid that climbs walls and escapes its container by flowing uphill. It is visually eerie and counterintuitive, which makes it captivating for kids. The effect looks like magic but is real quantum mechanics at macroscopic scale.

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** Second lightest element; so light it escapes Earth's atmosphere into space
- **Reactivity:** Completely non-reactive — a noble gas with a full electron shell
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Lowest boiling point of ANY element (-269C); cannot be frozen into a solid at normal pressure even at absolute zero
- **Appearance:** Colorless, odorless, invisible gas; glows pale peach-yellow in discharge tubes
- **Other:** The only element first discovered on the Sun (via its spectral lines) before being found on Earth

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Helium is the only element that was discovered in space before it was found on Earth — scientists spotted it in sunlight in 1868
2. When cooled to near absolute zero, helium becomes a "superfluid" that can climb up walls and escape from any container — like a liquid ghost
3. Earth is slowly losing its helium forever — once helium floats to the top of the atmosphere, it escapes into space and never comes back
