# Copper (Cu) — Element 29

## Video Recommendation
**Should generate video:** Yes
**Confidence:** High
**Video concept:** Copper wire held in a flame, producing a vivid bright green-blue flame as the copper burns

## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up of a coiled copper wire being held in the flame of a Bunsen burner. The copper's distinctive warm rose-gold surface begins to glow red, then the flame erupts into a spectacular vivid green-blue color. The brilliant emerald-green flames lick and dance around the copper, producing an intense, saturated green light that illuminates everything nearby. The copper glows orange-red at the point of contact while the surrounding flame blazes bright green, creating a stunning contrast of complementary colors. Green sparks and wisps of emerald-colored fire swirl upward. The copper surface shifts from its natural warm pink-orange to iridescent blue-purple oxidation colors. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Copper's green flame test is one of the most vivid and recognizable in chemistry — the bright emerald-green color is unmistakable and dramatic. Combined with copper's unique natural rose-gold color (it's one of only two non-gray metals, along with gold), the contrast between the warm metal and the cool green flame creates a visually stunning composition. This is a classic demonstration that immediately communicates the identity of copper.

## Alternative Video Concepts Considered
- **Molten copper being poured:** Dramatic but loses copper's distinctive color since it glows orange like most molten metals
- **Copper developing green patina (verdigris):** Iconic but way too slow for 8 seconds, even time-lapsed
- **Polished copper showing its rose-gold color:** Beautiful but too static

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 8.96 g/cm³ — dense and heavy
- **Reactivity:** Slowly develops a green patina (verdigris) when exposed to air and moisture over time
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at 1,085°C
- **Appearance:** Distinctive warm rose-gold/orange-pink color — one of only two metals that aren't silvery-gray (the other is gold)
- **Other:** One of the best conductors of electricity and heat; humans have used copper for over 10,000 years

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Copper is one of only two metals in the whole periodic table that has a color other than silvery-gray — it's a warm pinkish-orange, and the other colorful metal is gold!
2. The Statue of Liberty is covered in copper — it was originally shiny copper-colored when it was built, but over 20 years it turned green from a chemical reaction with the air.
3. Copper burns with a brilliant bright green flame, which is why green fireworks almost always contain copper compounds.
