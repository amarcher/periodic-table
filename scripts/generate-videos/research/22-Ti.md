# Titanium (Ti) — Element 22

## Video Recommendation
**Should generate video:** Yes
**Confidence:** High
**Video concept:** Titanium rod held against a grinding wheel, throwing off a spectacular shower of brilliant white sparks

## Video Prompt (for Veo 3.1 Fast)
Extreme close-up of a titanium metal rod being pressed against a spinning grinding wheel in a dark workshop. An explosive shower of blindingly brilliant white sparks erupts from the contact point, spraying outward in a wide, dazzling fan. The sparks are intensely white and bright, distinctly different from the orange sparks of steel, bursting and branching as they fly through the air. The grinding wheel spins rapidly, sending continuous streams of white-hot titanium particles cascading through the darkness like a fountain of tiny stars. Sparks bounce and ricochet off surfaces, each one burning with pure white intensity. Dark background, cinematic lighting, extreme close-up, no text, no watermarks, no logos.

## Why This Concept
Titanium's distinctive brilliant white sparks when ground are both visually spectacular and scientifically interesting — the white color comes from titanium's high affinity for oxygen combined with low thermal conductivity, causing tiny particles to burn white-hot in air. This spark test is actually how metalworkers identify titanium in the real world. The blinding white shower against a dark background creates an unforgettable, dramatic visual.

## Alternative Video Concepts Considered
- **Titanium being anodized (rainbow colors):** Beautiful but requires an electrochemical setup that's hard to convey in 8 seconds
- **Polished titanium reflecting light:** Too static, no dramatic action
- **Titanium resisting corrosion:** Invisible process, not visually interesting

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 4.51 g/cm³ — strong as steel but 45% lighter
- **Reactivity:** Resists corrosion incredibly well; even seawater can't corrode it for thousands of years
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Very high melting point of 1,668°C
- **Appearance:** Silvery-gray lustrous metal with excellent strength-to-weight ratio
- **Other:** Named after the Titans of Greek mythology; used in spacecraft, jet engines, and artificial hip joints

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Titanium makes brilliant white sparks when you grind it — so bright they can hurt your eyes, and that's actually how metalworkers tell titanium apart from other metals!
2. Titanium is as strong as steel but almost half the weight — that's why it's used to build fighter jets, spacecraft, and even replacement bones for people.
3. Titanium dioxide is the white pigment in most white paint, sunscreen, and even the white coating on M&M candies!
