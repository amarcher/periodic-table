## Why This Concept
Strontium produces the most iconic red color in all of chemistry — it is the element responsible for the brilliant red in fireworks and emergency road flares. The deep crimson flame is immediately visually striking and unmistakable. Kids will instantly connect this to every red firework they have ever seen.

## Interesting Properties for App Display
- **Density:** 2.64 g/cm3, a relatively light alkaline earth metal
- **Reactivity:** Reacts with water and burns readily in air, must be stored under oil
- **Melting/Boiling Point:** Melts at 777C, boils at 1377C
- **Appearance:** Silvery-white metal that rapidly turns yellowish in air due to oxide formation
- **Other:** Named after Strontian, a village in Scotland where it was discovered in a lead mine

## Kid-Friendly Factoids
1. Every red firework you have ever seen gets its brilliant red color from strontium compounds — it is the king of red
2. Emergency road flares burn bright red because they contain strontium nitrate
3. Strontium is so reactive that freshly cut metal will turn yellow within minutes as it reacts with oxygen in the air
